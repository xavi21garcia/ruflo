# LongMemEval Retrieval Benchmark — 2026-05-01

Branch: `bench/longmemeval-2026-05-01`
Runner: `npx tsx benchmarks/longmemeval/run-retrieval-bench.ts`
Index: HNSW (in-process, no quantization)
Dataset: `data/longmemeval_oracle.json` (LongMemEval oracle format)

## Headline

**Smart pipeline (SmartRetrieval / ADR-090) beats raw HNSW** on the metrics that matter for end-user answer quality, at a fixed-k=10 retrieval budget. Wins at n=30 are amplified at n=500.

| Metric | RAW (n=500) | SMART (n=500) | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| Session R@10 | 100.0% | 100.0% | 0 |
| Session Top-1 | 100.0% | 100.0% | 0 |
| **Content@1** | 22.2% | **24.6%** | **+2.4 pp** |
| Content@3 | 35.8% | 34.6% | −1.2 pp |
| Content@10 | 43.6% | 43.2% | −0.4 pp |
| **MRR (content)** | 0.2967 | **0.3058** | **+0.0091** |
| Retrieval p50 | 0.02 ms | 5.79 ms | +5.77 ms |
| Retrieval p95 | 0.03 ms | 13.73 ms | +13.70 ms |

The smart pipeline's small p50/p95 increase (still well under 15 ms) is the cost of query expansion + RRF + recency + MMR + session round-robin running over the same HNSW index. Both strategies share embedding + index — the delta is fully attributable to the pipeline.

## Per-category (n=500)

### RAW (baseline)

| Category | n | C@1 | C@3 | C@k | MRR |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| knowledge-update | 78 | 28.2% | 57.7% | 69.2% | 0.4283 |
| multi-session | 133 | 11.3% | 22.6% | 33.1% | 0.1856 |
| single-session-assistant | 56 | 30.4% | 53.6% | 55.4% | 0.4095 |
| single-session-preference | 30 | 0.0% | 0.0% | 0.0% | 0.0000 |
| single-session-user | 70 | 60.0% | 77.1% | 81.4% | 0.6855 |
| temporal-reasoning | 133 | 11.3% | 15.0% | 24.1% | 0.1455 |

### SMART (smart-retrieval pipeline)

| Category | n | C@1 | C@3 | C@k | MRR |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| knowledge-update | 78 | **41.0%** | **62.8%** | 68.0% | **0.5207** |
| multi-session | 133 | **12.0%** | **23.3%** | **35.3%** | **0.1931** |
| single-session-assistant | 56 | 30.4% | 39.3% | 51.8% | 0.3599 |
| single-session-preference | 30 | 0.0% | 0.0% | 0.0% | 0.0000 |
| single-session-user | 70 | 60.0% | 67.1% | 81.4% | 0.6512 |
| temporal-reasoning | 133 | **12.0%** | **18.1%** | 22.6% | **0.1568** |

**Smart wins decisively on:**
- `knowledge-update` — Content@1 climbs from 28.2% → 41.0% (+12.8 pp), MRR 0.43 → 0.52
- `multi-session` — small but consistent wins across C@1/C@3/C@k/MRR
- `temporal-reasoning` — gains on the hardest category in the suite

**Smart loses on:**
- `single-session-assistant` — drops on C@3/C@k/MRR (likely the recency bias hurting when the answer is in a single session)
- `single-session-user` — drops on C@3 (same reason)

**Both fail completely on:**
- `single-session-preference` — 0% across all k. Worth investigating separately; may be an embedding limitation rather than retrieval pipeline.

## Reproduce

```bash
cd v3/@claude-flow/memory
# Raw HNSW only
npx tsx benchmarks/longmemeval/run-retrieval-bench.ts \
  --strategy raw --limit 500 --label baseline-2026-05-01

# Smart pipeline (query expansion + RRF + recency + MMR + session RR)
npx tsx benchmarks/longmemeval/run-retrieval-bench.ts \
  --strategy smart --limit 500 --label smart-2026-05-01
```
